The problem

My team didn't have a consistent way to surface concerns about psychological safety, and generic engagement surveys didn't fit our cadence. I wanted something specific to how we worked: anonymous, low-ceremony, and structured enough to turn conversation into action.

What I built

A structured team health check tool, built with Claude Code from prototype to deployed product.

Takeaway

What I learned

The real byproduct of building this wasn't the tool itself. It was hands-on fluency with AI-assisted prototyping. I now have a much sharper sense of what's prototypable quickly and what isn't, and that changes how I scope and validate ideas with engineering teams in my day job. A PM who has built something under constraint talks differently to engineering teams than one who hasn't.

Prototype fluency changes how you scope

Knowing what's fast to build vs. what isn't is hard to develop without building. This project gave me that.

A PM who builds talks differently

The conversations I have with engineering teams are different now. I can estimate, I can scope, I can say "that's actually a two-line change" with conviction.
