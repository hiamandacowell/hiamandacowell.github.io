About the thesis

Topological Data Analysis (TDA) combines topology, the mathematical study of shape, with data analysis. The thesis introduces the foundational tools of TDA, including simplicial complexes, homology, and persistent homology, and discusses how these tools are used to study the shape of data in fields like medical imaging and autonomous navigation.

Topology — shape, not numbers

Topology studies the properties of shapes that survive bending, stretching, or folding without tearing. To topology, two shapes are "the same" if one can be deformed into the other. The concrete invariant TDA uses most is the number of holes.

Simplicial complexes — building shapes from pieces

To analyze a dataset's shape, TDA first builds a structure called a simplicial complex from the data. The pieces are simple: points, line segments, triangles, tetrahedra, and higher-dimensional analogs.

Persistent homology — tracking shape across scale

Real-world data is noisy, so TDA examines a simplicial complex at many scales at once. Cycles appear and disappear as the complex grows. Features that vanish quickly are noise; features that persist across many scales are real.
